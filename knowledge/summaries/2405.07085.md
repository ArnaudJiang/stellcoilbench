# Optimised stellarators with a positive radial electric field

**Authors:** Per Helander, Alan G. Goodman, Craig D. Beidler, Michal Kuczyński, Håkan M. Smith | **Year:** 2024 | **arXiv:** 2405.07085

## Summary

This paper explores stellarator optimization for a positive radial electric field \(E_r > 0\) in the core, which expels impurities via neoclassical transport and can create a turbulent transport barrier at the electron–ion root transition. Criterion for electron root: \(x = D_e^{1/\nu}/D_i^{\sqrt{\nu}} \gtrsim 1\) implies \(\tau \gtrsim \tau_* \propto M^{1/7} c_{\sqrt{\nu}}^{2/7}(\nu_{\ast i}/\epsilon_{\text{eff}}\rho_{\ast i})^{3/7}\). By tailoring magnetic geometry so that barely trapped particles (close to trapped–passing boundary) are omnigenous while deeply trapped are not, \(c_{\sqrt{\nu}}\) can be reduced relative to \(\epsilon_{\text{eff}}\), lowering \(\tau_*\) and allowing electron root at \(\tau=1\). Cary–Shasharina criterion \(\partial F/\partial\alpha=0\) is extended to partial omnigenity (shallowly trapped omnigenous, deeply trapped not). An explicit QI configuration is optimized with modified QI target (eq. 15) and filamentary-coil feasibility constraint; it exhibits positive \(E_r\) in the core under reactor conditions. Transition region between electron and ion roots has strongly sheared flow; Waltz criterion \(dE_r/dr \gtrsim \gamma_{\max}\) can suppress turbulence and create a transport barrier.

## Optimization Advice for StellCoilBench

- For impurity expulsion: target positive \(E_r\) in core by minimizing \(c_{\sqrt{\nu}}/\epsilon_{\text{eff}}^{3/2}\); favors shallowly trapped omnigenity.
- Electron root at \(\tau=1\) requires \(\epsilon_{\text{eff}}\) small and \(c_{\sqrt{\nu}}\) very small; optimize for orbits near trapped–passing boundary.
- QI target can be modified to emphasize shallowly trapped omnigenity; sample multiple \(y\) (bounce-point \(B\)) and \(\alpha\).
- Sheared-flow region at electron–ion root transition may suppress turbulence; width \(w \lesssim \rho_i/\alpha\) for barrier.
- Include ambipolarity/\(E_r\) in transport metrics for reactor-relevant case design.
- Ion \(\sqrt{\nu}\) transport scales as \(D_i^{\sqrt{\nu}} \propto c_{\sqrt{\nu}} \nu_i^{1/2}/\omega_E^{3/2}\); reducing \(c_{\sqrt{\nu}}\) lowers ion transport and eases electron root.

## Takeaways

- Positive \(E_r\) in stellarator core is achievable by geometry optimization; enables impurity expulsion without \(T_e \gg T_i\).
- Partial omnigenity for shallowly trapped particles is key; differs from full omnigenity.
- Electron–ion root transition can create a transport barrier via flow shear; improves core confinement.
