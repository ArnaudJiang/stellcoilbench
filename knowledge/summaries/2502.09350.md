# Optimizing omnigenity like quasisymmetry for stellarators

**Authors:** Hengqian Liu, Guodong Yu, Caoxiang Zhu, Ge Zhuang | **Year:** 2025 | **arXiv:** 2502.09350

## Summary

This paper introduces a novel method for optimizing omnigenity that unifies both quasisymmetric (QS) and non-QS omnigenity optimization using a coordinate transformation analogous to Cary–Shasharina. Omnisymmetry is achieved by reducing asymmetric modes in a modified coordinate system rather than directly optimizing the second adiabatic invariant \( \mathcal{J} \). The approach was implemented in SIMSOPT using trust-region reflective optimization over plasma boundary Fourier coefficients \(\{R_{m,n},Z_{m,n}\}\). They report precisely omnigenous configurations in toroidal (TO), poloidal (PO), and helical (HO) variants with aspect ratios 6.0 (TO), 6.5 (PO), and 8.0 (HO)—lower than prior designs (19.6, 8.0, 18.3). They also demonstrate direct optimization of pseudosymmetry (PS) and piecewise omnigenity (pwO). Omnigenity residues are two orders lower than W7-X high-mirror and comparable to Landreman–Paul QA. Alpha particle loss fractions at ARIES-CS scale are 0.50% (TO), 0.16% (HO), and 0% (PO). The method can start from a circular torus and is compatible with STELLOPT, SIMSOPT, and DESC.

## Optimization Advice for StellCoilBench

- Use aspect ratio targets \( A_* = 6 \)–\( 8 \) for compact omnigenity; TO achieves \( A = 6 \), PO \( A = 6.5 \), HO \( A = 8 \).
- Target rotational transforms \( \iota \sim 0.7 \) (QA-like) or \( \iota \sim 1.15 \) (QH-like); constant-\( \iota \) profiles help convergence.
- For TO/HO, use QA or QH as a warm start; cold starts from a circular torus work but are slower.
- Add aspect ratio and rotational transform penalties (\( f_{\text{ap}} \), \( f_\iota \)) to the omnigenity objective; trust-region reflective is robust.
- Consider piecewise omnigenity (pwO) for reduced elongation and good confinement; pwO can achieve ~1% alpha loss at reactor scale with less shaping than full omnigenity.

## Takeaways

- Non-QS omnigenity can be optimized with simplicity comparable to QS by minimizing asymmetric components in a modified coordinate system.
- Toroidal and helical omnigenity enable compact aspect ratios (6–8) compared to existing designs.
- Pseudosymmetry and piecewise omnigenity offer engineering flexibility (lower elongation) with acceptable transport; PS has poor alpha confinement despite low neoclassical transport.
