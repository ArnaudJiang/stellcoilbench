# Collisionless zonal-flow dynamics in quasisymmetric stellarators

**Authors:** Hongxuan Zhu, Z. Lin, A. Bhattacharjee | **Year:** 2024 | **arXiv:** 2407.19592

## Summary

This paper studies the linear collisionless response to zonal-density perturbations in QS stellarators: GAM oscillations and the Rosenbluth–Hinton (RH) residual flow. In QA stellarators, GAM behavior is similar to tokamaks; in QH stellarators with small effective safety factor \(q_N = (\iota - N)^{-1}\), GAM oscillations vanish and the initial perturbation relaxes without GAM. The RH residual is modified by a geometric factor \(\mathcal{C}\) from flux-surface-averaged polarization \(\langle n_i m_i |\nabla\psi|^2/B^2 \rangle\); an analytic expression for \(\mathcal{C}\) is derived using the near-axis expansion. For QA, \(\mathcal{C}>1\) can enhance the RH residual; for QH, \(\mathcal{C}<1\) but the residual is still large due to small \(|q_N|\). GTC simulations validate the theory for precise QA and QH (Landreman–Paul); agreement requires small QS-breaking magnetic components. Theory fails for 1st-order NAE configurations with significant QS error.

## Optimization Advice for StellCoilBench

- High-precision QS (e.g., Landreman–Paul) is needed for RH-level predictions; 1st-order NAE has too much QS error.
- QH configurations with small \(|q_N|\) have enhanced zonal-flow residual, potentially favorable for Dimits shift and turbulence suppression.
- QA stellarators can achieve \(\mathcal{C}>1\) without vertical-stability limits; QH typically has \(\mathcal{C}<1\).
- For zonal-flow–turbulence studies, use equilibria with QS-breaking components small relative to dominant QS modes.
- \(\mathcal{C}\) depends on axis shape, \(\bar{\eta}\), and \(\sigma\); can be estimated from NAE or computed numerically.

## Takeaways

- Zonal-flow dynamics in QS stellarators differ from tokamaks via geometry-dependent \(\mathcal{C}\) and effective safety factor.
- GAM oscillations exist in QA but not in QH when \(q_N/\sqrt{\mathcal{C}}<1\); RH residual can be larger in QH.
- Good agreement with theory requires QS quality comparable to precise QA/QH designs.
