# A family of quasi-axisymmetric stellarators with varied rotational transform

**Authors:** Stefan Buller, Matt Landreman, John Kappel, Rahul Gaur | **Year:** 2024 | **arXiv:** 2401.09021

## Summary

This paper applies continuation in \( \bar{\iota}_* \) to the Landreman–Paul Precise QA, generating a family of \( n_{\text{fp}} = 2 \) QA equilibria with \( \bar{\iota} \in [0.12, 0.75] \). They optimize \( f = f_{QS} + (A - A_*)^2 + (\bar{\iota} - \bar{\iota}_*)^2 \) using SIMSOPT (Trust Region Reflective) and VMEC. Quasisymmetry error is lowest for \( \bar{\iota} \in [0.2, 0.65] \) (\( \sim 2.4\times 10^{-8} \)–\( 9\times 10^{-8} \)). Fast-particle losses (SIMPLE, 5000 alphas, 0.2 s) decrease with \( \bar{\iota} \) until \( \bar{\iota} \approx 0.73 \), where QS degradation dominates. Coil–plasma distance (Regcoil, \( K_{\max} = 17.16 \) MA/m, \( B_{n,\text{RMS}} = 0.01 \) T) is 2.85–3.25 m at ARIES-CS scale with a minimum near \( \bar{\iota} \sim 0.35 \). ITG linear growth rates (Stella) increase with \( \bar{\iota} \) and shift to higher \( k_y \); nonlinear heat flux (GX) is non-monotonic. Positive shear increases growth rates and heat flux; shear scan shows \( \bar{\hat{s}} > 0.085 \) is destabilizing.

## Optimization Advice for StellCoilBench

- Use continuation in \( \bar{\iota} \) (steps ~0.01) from a known QA optimum to explore \( \iota \) space efficiently.
- Sweet spot for QA: \( \bar{\iota} \in [0.2, 0.65] \) gives best QS; higher \( \bar{\iota} \) increases elongation and limits achievability.
- Target \( \bar{\iota} \sim 0.35 \) for minimum coil–plasma distance at fixed accuracy.
- Avoid \( \iota \) near rationals for single-flux-tube turbulence metrics; use multiple \( \alpha \) or max over flux tubes.
- Limit positive shear targets; \( \bar{\hat{s}} > 0.085 \) degrades ITG stability.

## Takeaways

- Continuation systematically explores \( \iota \) trade-offs; landscape has flat/insensitive modes so non-uniqueness is expected.
- Fast-particle confinement improves with \( \bar{\iota} \) up to a point; orbit-width scaling \( \propto 1/\iota \) is qualitative only.
- Coil–plasma distance varies by ~±10% across the family; aspect ratio and \( \iota \) both matter.
