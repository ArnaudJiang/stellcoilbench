# The topology of non-resonant stellarator divertors

**Authors:** Robert Davies, Christopher B. Smiet, Alkesh Punjabi, Allen Boozer, Sophia A. Henneberg | **Year:** 2025 | **arXiv:** 2501.18293

## Summary

This paper applies topological methods (winding numbers of closed contours, Jacobian of fixed points) to understand diversion in non-resonant divertors (NRD). In the NRD Hamiltonian, diversion is driven by "unpaired" X-points—fixed points with no corresponding O-points—whose manifolds guide trajectories away from the confined region. Four \(\iota=0\) X-points are found; they occur where \(d\theta/d\xi = d\psi_t/d\xi = 0\). A similar axisymmetric Hamiltonian shows the same unpaired-X-point behavior. The QUASR database is scanned via winding-number calculations over the maximal contractible \(n\)-mapping set; ~34 of ~100 configs reported \(W<1\), indicating unpaired X-points. Three examples: (1) config 104183—tokamak-like \(\iota=0\) X-points at top/bottom, no plasma current; (2) config 74609—four unpaired \(\iota=1\) X-points, \(A=5\); (3) config 1258083—\(\iota=0\) X-point plus 4/4 island chain with hyper-hyperbolic (positive-index) fixed points. Winding number \(W<+1\) implies more X-points than O-points; unpaired X-point manifolds can leave the mapping set and provide efficient diversion.

## Optimization Advice for StellCoilBench

- Scan stellarator edge topologies via winding numbers over field-line Poincaré maps; \(W<1\) signals unpaired X-points and potential NRD behavior.
- Unpaired \(\iota=0\) X-points can yield tokamak-like diversion without plasma current; useful for QA edge design.
- QUASR (and similar databases) can be mined for divertor-favorable topologies; automated winding-number schemes enable large scans.
- For divertor placement: X-point manifolds define outgoing flux channels; wall/PFC design should intersect these structures.
- Hyper-hyperbolic fixed points (positive index, hyperbolic geometry) in island chains are a novel edge topology worth characterizing.

## Takeaways

- NRD diversion is topologically explained by unpaired X-points; their manifolds define the flux channels.
- Unpaired X-points appear in realistic stellarators (QUASR); they are a general feature, not limited to toy Hamiltonians.
- Topological methods (winding numbers, Greene's residue, Lyapunov exponents) can classify and optimize divertor geometries.
