# Flexible Stellarator Physics Facility

**Authors:** F. I. Parra, S.-G. Baek, M. Churchill, D. R. Demers, B. Dudson, N. M. Ferraro, B. Geiger, S. Gerhardt, K. C. Hammond, S. Hudson, R. Jorge, E. Kolemen, D. M. Kriete, S. T. A. Kumar, M. Landreman, C. Lowe, D. A. Maurer, F. Nespoli, N. Pablant, M. J. Pueschel, A. Punjabi, J. A. Schwartz, C. P. S. Swanson, A. M. Wright | **Year:** 2024 | **arXiv:** 2407.04039

## Summary

This is a facility proposal for a mid-scale US stellarator user facility to validate theoretical advances in confinement, turbulence, fast-ion physics, and non-resonant divertors. Two-stage design: Stage 1 (Exploration)—flexible magnetic configurations (QA and/or QH) to optimize for turbulent transport, fast-ion confinement, and NRD strike-line geometry; Stage 2 (Exploitation)—high-power core-edge integration with closed divertor, PFCs, and impurity control. Target parameters: \(\rho^*<10^{-2}\), \(\nu^*<10^{-2}\), \(T>1\) keV, \(a\sim0.5\) m; example point \(R=3\) m, \(B=2.5\) T, \(A=5\), \(P_{\mathrm{inj}}=6\) MW yields \(n_{e,0}\sim1.5\times10^{20}\) m\(^{-3}\), \(T_{i,0}\sim3.5\) keV, \(\beta\sim1.3\%\). Flexibility via modular coils + trim coils, or alternatives (2D continuous metal coils, dipole arrays). NRD testing is a primary mission. Budget range $180M–$720M; readiness: significant challenges before construction. Complements W7-X (which cannot test new QA/QH equilibria) and supports private fusion (Type One Energy, Thea Energy, Stellarex).

## Optimization Advice for StellCoilBench

- NRD strike-line resilience is a key validation target; StellCoilBench cases that probe edge/divertor topology align with facility mission.
- Target \(\rho^*<10^{-2}\), \(\nu^*<10^{-2}\) for reactor-relevant fast-ion and neoclassical regimes in case design.
- Config flexibility (QA + QH in one device) would test both symmetry classes; case proposals can span QA and QH.
- Critical validation gaps: turbulence optimization, fast-ion confinement, NRD; benchmark cases should address these.
- Pathfinder option: \(R=2\) m, \(B=1.7\) T, 6 MW for turbulence/divertor-only studies with relaxed \(\rho^*\), \(\nu^*\).

## Takeaways

- No existing facility can test recent QS/turbulence/NRD advances at scale; a new US mid-scale stellarator would fill this gap.
- Two-stage approach separates configuration exploration from high-power divertor integration.
- NRD testing in devices with bootstrap current is a stated priority; island divertor limits motivate NRD research.
