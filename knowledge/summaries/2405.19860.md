# Quasi-isodynamic stellarators with low turbulence as fusion reactor candidates

**Authors:** Alan G. Goodman, Pavlos Xanthopoulos, Gabriel G. Plunk, Håkan Smith, Carolin Nührenberg, Craig D. Beidler, Sophia A. Henneberg, Gareth Roberg-Clark, Michael Drevlak, Per Helander | **Year:** 2024 | **arXiv:** 2405.19860

## Summary

This paper presents Stable Quasi-Isodynamic Designs (SQuIDs): QI stellarators optimized with explicit ITG turbulence minimization. The ITG target minimizes \(|\nabla s|\) in regions of bad curvature (\(\tilde{\kappa}<0\)), reducing flux-surface compression where ITG is driven. Optimization uses SIMSOPT/VMEC with targets: \(f_{\mathrm{QI}}\), \(f_{\mathrm{max}\mathcal{J}}\) (\(T_{\mathcal{J}}=-0.06\)), \(f_{\nabla s}\) (bad-curvature \(|\nabla s|\)), mirror ratio, aspect ratio, \(\beta\), \(\iota\). SQuID vs. elongated QI (no \(f_{\nabla s}\)): SQuID has lower max \(a_{\mathrm{min}}|\nabla s|\) in bad curvature (~2.3 vs. ~6.5), slightly worse \(f_{\mathrm{QI}}\), excellent fast-ion confinement (zero loss at reactor scale), low \(\varepsilon_{\mathrm{eff}}^{3/2}\), max-\(\mathcal{J}\) for deeply trapped particles, and low bootstrap current. ITG linear growth rates and nonlinear heat flux are significantly reduced vs. W7-X. Both configs: \(A\simeq10\), \(\beta=2\%\), \(\iota\simeq[0.80,0.95]\).

## Optimization Advice for StellCoilBench

- For QI/turbulence cases: add ITG target \(f_{\nabla s} \propto \int \xi \Theta(\xi_{95}-\xi)\) with \(\xi=(a_{\mathrm{min}}\Theta(-\tilde{\kappa})|\nabla s|)^2\).
- Minimize \(|\nabla s|\) in bad curvature regions; expanding flux surfaces there reduces ITG drive.
- Max-\(\mathcal{J}\) target \(T_{\mathcal{J}}\sim -0.06\) improves fast-ion confinement and MHD stability.
- SQuID shows that ITG optimization is compatible with QI and max-\(\mathcal{J}\); trade-off is slightly reduced QI quality.
- Sample flux tubes at \(\theta=\phi=0\) (typical ITG location) for turbulence metrics; use both core (\(s=0.09\)) and half-radius (\(s=0.5\)).

## Takeaways

- First QI stellarator with explicit ITG minimization; demonstrates compatibility of QI, max-\(\mathcal{J}, and turbulence reduction.
- Flux-surface compression in bad curvature is a key ITG driver; reducing it yields lower heat flux.
- SQuID achieves reactor-relevant fast-ion confinement, neoclassical transport, and bootstrap current.
