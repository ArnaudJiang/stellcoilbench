# Omnigenous umbilic stellarators

**Authors:** R. Gaur, D. Panici, T. M. Elder, M. Landreman, K. E. Unalmis, Y. Elmacioglu, D. Dudt, R. Conlin, E. Kolemen | **Year:** 2025 | **arXiv:** 2505.04211

## Summary

This paper defines umbilic stellarators—equilibria with a continuous high-curvature edge on the boundary—and optimizes them for omnigenity using DESC. They parametrize the umbilic shape with floor-function sharp edges and fit to Fourier–Zernike; curvature is imposed along an umbilic curve. The objective combines aspect ratio, boundary \( \iota \approx n_{\text{FP}} m/n \), omnigenity/effective ripple, and umbilic curvature. US131 (vacuum, \( n_{\text{FP}}=1, n=3, m=1 \)) achieves \( \epsilon_{\text{eff}}^{3/2} < 0.001 \) for \( \rho \leq 0.6 \); the field is piecewise omnigenous rather than QS. Modular coil design for US131 yields 32 coils with high curvature; single-stage optimization of plasma + umbilic coil can simplify tokamak-to-stellarator conversion. Umbilic coils (helical, fractional helicity) can create cusp-like divertor regions.

## Optimization Advice for StellCoilBench

- For divertor/edge control, consider umbilic boundary shapes; they naturally favor piecewise omnigenity.
- Use \( w_O = 0 \), \( w_R \neq 0 \) (effective ripple only) to allow piecewise omnigenity without forcing a specific helicity.
- Umbilic equilibria need more coils per field period than standard designs; single-stage or umbilic-coil approaches can reduce coil complexity.
- Fit sharp umbilic boundaries with high Fourier–Zernike resolution; smoothing is inherent—optimize for high curvature, not perfect sharpness.
- Sensitivity analysis with dummy axis currents tests edge robustness for divertor placement.

## Takeaways

- Umbilic stellarators link boundary curvature to edge field structure; useful for X-point–/island-divertor design.
- Piecewise omnigenity can achieve low \( \epsilon_{\text{eff}} \) without quasisymmetry.
- Umbilic coils offer an alternative to full modular sets for creating ridge/divertor structures.
