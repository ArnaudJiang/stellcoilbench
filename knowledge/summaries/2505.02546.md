# Piecewise omnigenous stellarators with zero bootstrap current

**Authors:** Ivan Calvo, Jose Luis Velasco, Per Helander, Felix I. Parra | **Year:** 2025 | **arXiv:** 2505.02546

## Summary

This letter proves that piecewise omnigenous (pwO) fields can have zero bootstrap current at low collisionality for arbitrary density and temperature profiles—a property previously known only for quasi-isodynamic fields. The key condition is \(\Delta = \langle\chi_1\rangle/(\iota-a_1) + \langle\chi_2\rangle/(\iota-a_2) = 0\), where regions 1 and 2 are defined by parallelogram sides of types 1 and 2, and \(a_i\) are slopes of sides. For prototypical pwO fields (\(B=B_M\) in parallelogram, \(B=B_m\) outside), \(F_s\) is computed explicitly; \(\Delta=0\) implies \(\langle J_{\parallel}B\rangle \equiv 0\). In the parametrization of Velasco et al., \(\Delta \propto (\pi - w_2)\); varying \(w_2\) while keeping \(\iota\) constant tunes \(\Delta\). MONKES neoclassical simulations confirm \(D_{31}^*\) vanishes at \(\Delta=0\) across collisionality; for \(q=6\) smooth pwO, \(D_{31}^*\) at low \(\nu^*\) is smaller than W7-X HM. Zero bootstrap enables island-divertor compatibility.

## Optimization Advice for StellCoilBench

- For pwO fields: enforce \(\Delta = \langle\chi_1\rangle/(\iota-a_1) + \langle\chi_2\rangle/(\iota-a_2) = 0\) to achieve zero bootstrap current.
- In Velasco parametrization: \(\Delta \propto (\pi - w_2)\); set \(w_2 = \pi\) (or tune \(w_2\) to satisfy \(\Delta=0\)) for zero bootstrap.
- Zero-bootstrap pwO is compatible with island divertors; use for cases requiring current-free operation.
- Prototypical pwO (sharp parallelogram) is the analytically tractable limit; smooth approximations (\(q\) finite) still give low \(D_{31}^*\).
- Include \(\Delta\) as an inequality or equality constraint in pwO optimization for reactor-relevant designs.

## Takeaways

- pwO fields can achieve both low neoclassical transport and zero bootstrap current—the two key properties of QI.
- \(\Delta=0\) is a geometric condition on parallelogram parameters; enables pwO as an alternative to QI for divertor-compatible designs.
- pwO + zero bootstrap broadens the stellarator reactor design space beyond strict QI.
