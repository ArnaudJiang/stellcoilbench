# Exploring the operational limits of poloidal and helical arrays of Mirnov coils in stellarators by means of a synthetic diagnostic
**Authors:** P. Pons-Villalonga, Á. Cappa, J. Martínez-Fernández, O. S. Kozachok, E. Ascasíbar, TJ-II Team | **Year:** 2024 | **arXiv:** 2409.04221

## Summary

Synthetic diagnostic for Mirnov coils (magnetic fluctuation sensors) in non-axisymmetric configurations. TJ-II: 25-coil poloidal array (poloidal B component) + two 32-sensor helical tri-axial sub-arrays. Models Alfvén-mode potential perturbations; integrates induced currents → B oscillations at coil locations. 3D Lomb periodogram for mode number (m,n) identification. Findings: core-localized modes indistinguishable; low-n, low-m often have off-by-one errors; optimal polarization basis for tri-axial analysis; plasma shielding (n=0) degrades mode identification. Less directly applicable to coil optimization; relevant for validation of coil-produced fields (mode structure from error fields).

## Optimization Advice for StellCoilBench

- Mirnov arrays are diagnostics, not shaping coils; paper informs B·n error field mode structure (m,n) that diagnostics can resolve.
- Core modes hard to distinguish; edge/divertor field structure more diagnosable.
- When designing cases: error-field (m,n) content affects both confinement and diagnostic interpretation; consider mode structure in B·n spectrum.
- Tri-axial helical arrays (like TJ-II) provide better toroidal/poloidal coverage than poloidal-only for 3D equilibria.

## Takeaways

- Synthetic diagnostics enable mode-identification limits to be explored before hardware deployment.
- Stellarator non-axisymmetry complicates (m,n) identification; helical arrays help.
- Plasma shielding reduces diagnosability of certain modes.
