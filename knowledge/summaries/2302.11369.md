# Direct Optimization of Fast-Ion Confinement in Stellarators

**Authors:** David Bindel, Matt Landreman, Misha Padidar | **Year:** 2023 | **arXiv:** 2302.11369

## Summary

This paper optimizes plasma boundary Fourier coefficients to minimize expected alpha particle energy loss, computed via collisionless guiding-center tracing in SIMSOPT/VMEC. The objective \( \mathcal{J} = \mathbb{E}[3.5 e^{-2\mathcal{T}/t_{\max}}] \) weights prompt losses more heavily. They use SAA (fixed particle ensemble) for smoother objectives and BOBYQA (derivative-free) to handle noise. Bound constraints on \( B \in [B_-^*, B_+] \) enforce mirror ratio \( r^* \leq 1.35 \). Warm-start strategy: optimize with \( (t_{\max}, n_{\text{mode}}) = (0.1\text{ ms}, 1) \), then (1 ms, 1), (1 ms, 2), (10 ms, 2), (10 ms, 3). Configurations A and B achieve volume loss fractions ~2.2% and s=0.25 loss fractions 0.46% and 0.94%, outperforming most designs except LP-QA, LP-QH, and Wistell-A. Surprisingly, reducing QS violation (gradient descent on \( Q_{m,n} \)) increases losses—locally, low-loss configs are not quasi-symmetric.

## Optimization Advice for StellCoilBench

- Use SAA (fixed particle sample) rather than regenerating particles each iteration for smoother gradients in derivative-free methods.
- Warm-start with short \( t_{\max} \) (0.1–1 ms) and low \( n_{\text{mode}} \), then increase; ~2000+ particles per evaluation for stable optimization.
- Constrain mirror ratio \( r^* \leq 1.35 \) to avoid unphysical QI-like configurations with closed B-contours.
- QS and direct loss optimization can conflict; configs with excellent confinement may not be QS—don’t assume QS is necessary.
- BOBYQA handles noisy objectives well; variance reduction (e.g., importance sampling) could cut required particles ~100×.

## Takeaways

- Direct fast-ion optimization is tractable (1–2 days on 48 cores) and yields <1% loss fractions without enforcing QS.
- QS and alpha confinement are not perfectly correlated; reducing QS violation can increase losses locally.
- Warm-starting over \( t_{\max} \) and \( n_{\text{mode}} \) is critical for convergence.
