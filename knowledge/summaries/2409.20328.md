# Near-axis description of stellarator-symmetric quasi-isodynamic stellarators to second order
**Authors:** Eduardo Rodríguez, Gabriel G. Plunk, Rogerio Jorge | **Year:** 2024 | **arXiv:** 2409.20328

## Summary

Theoretical extension of near-axis expansion to second order for stellarator-symmetric quasi-isodynamic (QI) fields. QI: poloidally closed |B| contours, omnigeneous but no symmetry direction. First order: elliptic cross-sections only; second order adds triangularity, MHD-relevant shaping. Half-helicity axes (κ=0 at points) require careful continuity/smoothness. Inverse-coordinate approach (Boozer); equilibrium + omnigeneity to O(r²). Numerical implementation; benchmarks vs global equilibria (minimally shaped vacuum, shaped finite-β, half-helicity, QI-optimized). Foundation for systematic QI exploration and optimization. No direct coil content; informs stage-1 target and near-axis seeds for coil design.

## Optimization Advice for StellCoilBench

- Second-order QI near-axis provides better seeds for QI coil optimization than first-order-only.
- Half-helicity (κ=0) QI designs need special treatment; verify continuity in near-axis constructions.
- Use near-axis QI as stage-1 target when designing QI cases; then stage-2 coil optimization.
- Triangularity, shaping at second order affect MHD stability and coil complexity—include in case design targets.

## Takeaways

- Near-axis QI theory to second order enables more realistic QI optimization workflows.
- Half-helicity QI is prominent in designs; theory now supports it consistently.
- Near-axis framework supports both theoretical understanding and practical optimization seeding.
