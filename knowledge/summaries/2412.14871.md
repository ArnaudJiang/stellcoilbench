# Exploration of the parameter space of piecewise omnigenous stellarator magnetic fields

**Authors:** J. L. Velasco, E. Sánchez, I. Calvo | **Year:** 2024 | **arXiv:** 2412.14871

## Summary

Piecewise omnigenous (pwO) fields reduce \(1/\nu\) neoclassical transport without requiring closed \(B\)-contours as in full omnigenity; the second adiabatic invariant \(J\) is piecewise constant with transitions between regions. This paper parametrizes pwO fields systematically. Simplest pwO: \(B=B_{\mathrm{max}}\) inside a parallelogram, \(B=B_0\) outside; rotational transform \(\iota=\iota_0\) must connect two corners by field lines. Construction uses \(B_{pwQS}\) (sharp parallelogram) and \(\iota_0(t_1,t_2,N_p w_1)\); stellarator symmetry constrains parallelogram center. Non-straight \(B\)-contours are introduced via \(\delta w_1\), \(\delta w_2\); constraints (e.g., \(\partial\delta w_1/\partial\theta < t_1+1/\iota\)) prevent tangency and preserve pwO. Hybrid QS+pwO fields combine omnigenity for deeply trapped particles with pwO for barely trapped. W7-X standard, LHD inward-shifted, and Bindel config A are argued to be nearly pwO. NCSX and CIEMAT-QI resemble pwO \(B\)-contours; parametrization enables including pwO as an explicit optimization target.

## Optimization Advice for StellCoilBench

- Consider piecewise omnigenity as a design target; fields like W7-X and LHD achieve low \(\varepsilon_{\mathrm{eff}}\) without full omnigenity.
- Parallelogram-shaped \(B_{\mathrm{max}}\) regions with \(\iota=\iota_0\) give exact pwO; small deviations still reduce \(1/\nu\) flux.
- For QI-like boundaries: combine QI for deeply trapped with pwO for barely trapped; reduces elongation vs. pure QI.
- Inspect \(B(\theta,\zeta)\) contours for parallelogram-like structure when assessing neoclassical quality.
- pwO parametrization (eqs. 7–8, 44–56) can be implemented in optimization codes analogous to Cary–Shasharina for omnigenity.

## Takeaways

- pwO broadens the configuration space for low neoclassical transport; exact omnigenity is not required.
- Systematic parametrization of pwO enables design-code integration and systematic exploration.
- W7-X standard, LHD, NCSX, and CIEMAT-QI illustrate the wide applicability of the pwO framework.
