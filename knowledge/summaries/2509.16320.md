# Exponential Spectral Scaling: Robust and Efficient Stellarator Boundary Optimization via Mode-Dependent Scaling

**Authors:** Byoungchan Jang, Rory Conlin, Matt Landreman | **Year:** 2025 | **arXiv:** 2509.16320

## Summary

This paper introduces Exponential Spectral Scaling (ESS) to address mode amplitude disparity (MAD) in stellarator boundary optimization. MAD—the 6–7 order dynamic range between low and high Fourier modes—causes poor conditioning and local minima. ESS applies \( x_{\text{scaled}} = x_{\text{original}} / \exp(-\alpha \, g(m,n)) \) with \( g = \max(|m|,|n|) \) (\( L_\infty \)) or \( L_1, L_2 \), reducing dynamic range to 2–3 orders. Decay rate \( \alpha \sim 1.1 \)–2.8 fits physical configs. ESS enables direct full-spectrum optimization without Fourier continuation staging, achieving 2–5× speedup and smoother convergence. Demonstrated on Landreman–Paul QA and QH with DESC and SIMSOPT; ESS outperforms both full-spectrum (no scaling) and Fourier continuation. Implement via scipy `x_scale` or explicit preconditioning.

## Optimization Advice for StellCoilBench

- Apply ESS with \( \alpha \in [1.1, 2.8] \) and \( L_\infty \) norm: \( x_{\text{scaled}} = x / \exp(-\alpha \max(|m|,|n|)) \).
- Use ESS instead of Fourier continuation for faster, single-stage optimization; avoids arbitrary staging decisions.
- Combine ESS with Jacobian-based scaling (`x_scale='jac'`) if available; ESS alone suffices when jac scaling is weak.
- Full-spectrum + ESS reaches similar or better minima than staged continuation; run-to-run variation is lower.
- Prefer \( L_\infty \) over \( L_1 \) for square (\( m,n \)) domains typical in VMEC/DESC.

## Takeaways

- Mode amplitude disparity is a central numerical obstacle; ESS provides a principled preconditioner aligned with physical spectral decay.
- ESS enables robust full-spectrum optimization without staging; 2–5× speedup with improved convergence.
- Compatible with SIMSOPT and DESC; can inform variable scaling for global optimization bounds.
