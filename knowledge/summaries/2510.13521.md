# Narrow Operator Models of Stellarator Equilibria in Fourier Zernike Basis
**Authors:** Timo Thun, Rory Conlin, Dario Panici, Daniel Böckenhoff | **Year:** 2025 | **arXiv:** 2510.13521

## Summary

This paper presents neural-network operator models that parametrize continuous families of ideal MHD equilibria in DESC's Fourier-Zernike basis. Two-layer MLPs map a scalar pressure multiplier η_p to the optimization subspace y (tangent to linear constraints), minimizing the sum of force residuals over I=10 equispaced η_p points. Models achieve volume-averaged normalized force error \(\langle\mathbf{F}\rangle_{\text{vol,norm}}\) comparable to or below DESC on interpolation, with good quasi-symmetry preservation for QA/QH cases. Tested equilibria include W7-X (L=M+1=7, M=6, N=6), Heliotron-like (M=9, N=3), quasi-helical with bootstrap current (M=8, N=8), and DIII-D (M=12, N=0). Key findings: narrow MLPs (e.g. 1→32→64→265 nodes) suffice; wider/deeper networks stagnate in local minima; direct force-residual training can beat DESC at some η_p (quasi-helical shows lower \(\langle\mathbf{F}\rangle\) at η_p∈[0.4,0.9]); extrapolation to η_p&gt;1 degrades monotonically. Training uses two stages (outliers first, then full I points), SELU activation, L-BFGS, and scaling \(y_{\text{scale}}\) from inverse sum of mode numbers. Training cost is 1–2 orders of magnitude higher than 10 DESC solves for non-axisymmetric cases; axisymmetric DIII-D is comparable. The work targets digital twins, real-time control, and pressure-uncertainty robustness.

## Optimization Advice for StellCoilBench

- **Resolution choices:** Use L=M, Zernike order L=M; for complex boundaries (e.g. W7-X), L=M+1 may improve Shafranov shift. Mode scaling 1/(l+|m|+|n|+1) reflects spectral decay and can improve convergence.
- **Force balance thresholds:** Target \(\langle\mathbf{F}\rangle_{\text{vol,norm}}&lt;1\%\) for equilibria used in coil optimization; post-processing should verify force balance separately from B·n.
- **Continuation:** Automatic continuation in η_b (boundary) then η_p (pressure) can avoid intermediate equilibria failures; some QH cases fail with η_p-first continuation.
- **Pressure sensitivity:** Operator-model findings imply optimization targets can be sensitive to pressure-profile uncertainty; consider varying pressure in case validation or robustness checks.
- **Equilibrium–coil coupling:** For cases relying on fixed-boundary equilibria, ensure continuation and resolution choices are consistent with downstream coil optimization requirements.

## Takeaways

- MLP-based equilibrium operators can interpolate equilibria across pressure scaling with force error competitive to DESC; extrapolation beyond training range is unreliable.
- Keeping MLP capacity narrow avoids local minima; similar guidance may apply to avoiding over-parameterization in optimization.
- Force residual is a valid direct optimization target and can sometimes outperform pre-computed equilibrium datasets.
- Continuation order (boundary vs pressure) and spectral resolution significantly affect convergence for QH and Heliotron-like equilibria.
