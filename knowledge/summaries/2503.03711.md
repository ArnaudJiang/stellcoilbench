# The shear Alfvén continuum of quasisymmetric stellarators

**Authors:** Elizabeth J. Paul, Abdullah Hyder, Eduardo Rodríguez, Rogério Jorge, Alexey Knyazev | **Year:** 2025 | **arXiv:** 2503.03711

## Summary

This paper develops a theoretical framework for the shear Alfvén wave (SAW) continuum in 3D quasisymmetric stellarators, relevant for energetic-particle-driven Alfvén eigenmodes. The continuum equation depends on \(|\nabla\psi|^2\) and \(B\); in QS stellarators, \(B\) is quasisymmetric but \(|\nabla\psi|^2\) is not, so the continuum differs from axisymmetric devices. Using the near-axis expansion and degenerate perturbation theory, gap formation is analyzed. Rotating ellipticity of flux surfaces drives a strong helical \((\delta m=2,\delta n=1)\) component; elongation drives EAE (QA) or HAE (2,2) (QH); toroidal variation of elongation drives MAE. Higher-order crossings and co-propagating modes can occur. Numerical validation on precise QA/QH and HSX; optimization to avoid passing-EP resonances by shifting gap locations is demonstrated. The framework enables incorporating Alfvénic stability into stellarator design.

## Optimization Advice for StellCoilBench

- SAW continuum gaps (TAE, EAE, HAE, MAE) depend on flux-surface shaping; near-axis \(\Psi_2\) captures dominant coupling.
- Rotating ellipticity drives HAE (2,1); elongation drives EAE (QA) or HAE (2,2) (QH); toroidal \(\kappa\), \(p\) variation drives MAE.
- For EP confinement: avoid aligning high-frequency gaps with passing-EP bounce/precession resonances; optimization can shift gaps.
- \(|\nabla\psi|^2\) spectral content (not just \(B\)) determines continuum; QS of \(B\) does not imply tokamak-like continuum.
- Near-axis model (Landreman et al.) gives good on-axis and qualitative mid-radius agreement for continuum structure.

## Takeaways

- QS stellarator continua differ from tokamaks due to \(|\nabla\psi|^2\) variation; flux-surface shaping drives multiple gap types.
- Gap structure can be optimized to reduce EP-driven AE instability risk.
- Near-axis expansion + perturbation theory provides an efficient continuum model for design.
