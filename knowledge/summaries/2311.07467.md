# Energetic Particle Tracing in Optimized Quasisymmetric Stellarator Equilibria

**Authors:** P. A. Figueiredo, R. Jorge, J. Ferreira, P. Rodrigues | **Year:** 2023 | **arXiv:** 2311.07467

## Summary

This paper compares guiding-center orbits and loss fractions in near-axis (pyQSC) vs. full MHD (VMEC) quasisymmetric equilibria. They use gyronimo (Euler–Lagrange) and SIMPLE (symplectic) for orbit integration. For QA, orbit agreement is good for passing particles; trapped particles show larger deviations at lower aspect ratio. For QH, passing–trapped transitions differ between NAE and VMEC, especially at low \( A \). They derive analytical expressions for orbit amplitude \( \Delta s \) and passing–trapped separatrix \( \lambda_s \) in the near-axis expansion. Loss fractions agree in the prompt-loss regime (\( t \lesssim 5\times 10^{-4} \) s for QA, \( A = 9.1 \)) but diverge afterward. NAE tracing is ~2 orders of magnitude faster than VMEC. QH exhibits smaller orbit widths (scaling with \( |\iota - N| \)) and faster divergence between NAE and VMEC loss fractions.

## Optimization Advice for StellCoilBench

- Use near-axis equilibria for rapid prompt-loss screening; expect agreement only for first-orbit losses.
- For QA at \( A \sim 9 \), NAE loss fractions match VMEC up to ~0.5 ms; use NAE for initial sweeps.
- QH has smaller banana widths; NAE is less reliable for QH loss extrapolation—verify with VMEC for critical cases.
- Trapped particles are most sensitive to NAE vs. MHD differences; passing particles are robust.
- Decay parameter \( \alpha \sim 1.6 \) and \( L_\infty \) norm align with physical spectral decay for ESS-style scaling.

## Takeaways

- Near-axis expansion can accelerate loss-fraction optimization for prompt losses by ~100×.
- NAE is most reliable for QA, moderate \( A \), and short time scales.
- Analytical orbit amplitude and separatrix estimates enable targeted sampling of passing vs. trapped populations.
