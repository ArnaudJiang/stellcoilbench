# Optimization of Nonlinear Turbulence in Stellarators

**Authors:** Patrick Kim, Stefan Buller, Rory Conlin, William Dorland, Daniel W. Dudt, Rahul Gaur, Rogerio Jorge, Egemen Kolemen, Matt Landreman, Noah R. Mandell, Dario Panici | **Year:** 2023 | **arXiv:** 2310.18842

## Summary

This paper directly optimizes stellarators for reduced nonlinear ITG heat flux by coupling the gyrokinetic code GX (GPU, minutes per run) with DESC. Because heat flux traces are noisy, they use SPSA (simultaneous perturbation stochastic approximation) for gradient estimation—only two objective evaluations per iteration. The workflow alternates: (1) minimize \( f_Q^2 + (A - A_{\text{target}})^2 \) with SPSA, (2) minimize \( f_{QS}^2 + (A - A_{\text{target}})^2 \) with automatic differentiation. Fourier continuation is used (start \( |m|,|n| \leq 1 \), incrementally expand). They achieve ~3× reduction in nonlinear heat flux at \( \rho = \sqrt{0.5} \) while preserving QH; improvement holds across \( \rho \) and \( \alpha \). Slight positive shear can reduce heat flux; too much shear increases it. T3D transport runs show ~10% temperature increase. Linear growth rates are poor proxies for nonlinear flux; quasilinear \( \gamma / \langle k_\perp^2 \rangle \) is inconsistent across \( k_x \).

## Optimization Advice for StellCoilBench

- For noisy objectives (turbulence, particle losses), use SPSA or similar; two evaluations per iteration is cheap compared to full gradients.
- Optimize at \( \rho = \sqrt{0.5} \) first; results often generalize to other radii, but multi-surface or multi-\( \alpha \) objectives improve uniformity.
- Target aspect ratio \( A = 8 \) for QH; include aspect ratio penalty to avoid drift.
- Slight positive shear can help ITG; avoid \( \iota \) near low-order rationals (e.g. 5/4) to reduce \( \alpha \)-variation artifacts.
- Stage objectives: turbulence first (SPSA), then QS (smooth); or reverse order; both can work.

## Takeaways

- Nonlinear gyrokinetic optimization is feasible with GPU codes and SPSA; GX + DESC enables ~20 h runs.
- Precise QH alone can worsen turbulence; combined turbulence–QS optimization is needed.
- Linear proxies (peak \( \gamma \), quasilinear) fail to predict nonlinear heat flux; direct nonlinear evaluation is necessary.
