# Exploration of the parameter space of quasisymmetric stellarator vacuum fields through adjoint optimisation

**Authors:** Richard Nies, Elizabeth J. Paul, Dario Panici, Stuart R. Hudson, Amitava Bhattacharjee | **Year:** 2024 | **arXiv:** 2404.02240

## Summary

This paper uses adjoint methods with SPEC (vacuum Laplace solver) to optimize quasisymmetry on the plasma boundary, reaching record levels of \( |B_{mn}|_{\infty}/B_{00} \sim 10^{-10} \) on the boundary. They optimize vacuum fields (not VMEC/DESC MHD) for robustness. The objective combines QS, aspect ratio, and rotational transform targets; optimization stages increment \( m_{\max}, n_{\max} \) from 1 and 3 up to ~10–20. BFGS with adjoint gradients requires \( \mathcal{O}(10^2) \) CPU-hours. QA at \( N_{\text{fp}} = 2 \) achieves \( A = 2.6 \); QA at \( N_{\text{fp}} = 3 \) reaches \( A = 4.5 \) with substantial magnetic shear (Greene residue optimization can restore integrability). QH at \( N_{\text{fp}} = 3,4 \) reaches \( A = 3.6 \) and \( A = 3 \). Rotational transform ranges: QA up to \( \iota_e \sim 0.7 \)–0.8, QH \( \iota_e \sim 1 \)–2. They use VMEC/SIMSOPT for volume refinement and integrability fixes.

## Optimization Advice for StellCoilBench

- Start with \( m_{\max} = 1, n_{\max} = 3 \) for QA (axis shape links to QS); then increase modes incrementally.
- Vacuum optimization is more robust than MHD for reaching very low QS; use VMEC only for post-processing and volume refinement.
- Low aspect ratio limits: \( A = 2.6 \) (QA \( N_{\text{fp}} = 2 \)), \( A = 3 \) (QH \( N_{\text{fp}} = 4 \)); lower \( A \) degrades QS.
- For \( N_{\text{fp}} = 3 \) QA, expect substantial shear and possible islands; add Greene residue minimization to restore nested surfaces.
- Adjoint methods enable \( \mathcal{O}(10^2) \) iterations over many stages; typical run ~\( 10^2 \) CPU-hours.

## Takeaways

- Adjoint-based vacuum optimization reaches extreme QS levels; boundary optimization approximates global QS.
- Compact aspect ratios (2.6–4.5) are achievable for QA and QH but require careful treatment of shear and integrability.
- \( N_{\text{fp}} = 3 \) QA has large shear; integrability can be recovered with targeted Greene residue optimization.
